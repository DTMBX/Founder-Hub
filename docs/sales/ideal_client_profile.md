# Ideal Client Profile (ICP)

> Last updated: 2026-02-17
> Classification: Internal — Sales
> Version: 1.0

---

## Summary

This document defines the ideal client profile for Evident
Technologies. It identifies the characteristics of law firms and
legal organizations most likely to benefit from the platform's
security posture, evidence integrity controls, and operational
infrastructure.

---

## Primary Segment: Mid-Size Litigation Firms

### Firm Characteristics

| Attribute | Profile |
|---|---|
| Size | 20–200 attorneys |
| Practice areas | Commercial litigation, employment law, intellectual property, regulatory defense |
| Discovery volume | Moderate to high (100+ matters/year or 10+ high-volume matters) |
| IT infrastructure | Limited in-house; reliant on outside vendors for forensic/technical services |
| Compliance awareness | Active but not yet formalized (no in-house CISO or GRC team) |
| Technology adoption | Pragmatic — adopts tools that reduce risk, not tools that promise transformation |

### Why This Segment

Mid-size litigation firms face a specific combination of pressures:

1. **Volume without infrastructure.** They handle enough discovery to
   face evidence integrity challenges, but lack the in-house forensic
   teams that large firms maintain.

2. **Reputational sensitivity.** A single evidence-handling failure
   can damage client relationships and create malpractice exposure.

3. **Procurement pragmatism.** They evaluate vendors on security
   posture and defensibility, not on brand recognition alone.

4. **Budget allocation.** They can justify technology expenditure
   when the risk-reduction case is clear and documented.

---

## Secondary Segment: High-Volume Discovery Firms

### Firm Characteristics

| Attribute | Profile |
|---|---|
| Size | Any (50+ attorneys typical) |
| Discovery volume | High (500+ matters/year or dedicated e-discovery practice) |
| Current tooling | Existing e-discovery platform (Relativity, Nuix, etc.) |
| Pain point | Export defensibility — concerned that current tools produce non-reproducible exports |
| Integration need | Complementary tool, not replacement — handles integrity and export verification |

### Why This Segment

High-volume discovery firms already have processing infrastructure
but may lack confidence in the defensibility of their export
pipeline. Evident addresses a specific gap: verifiable, deterministic
exports with cryptographic integrity.

---

## Tertiary Segment: Firms Without In-House Forensic Infrastructure

### Firm Characteristics

| Attribute | Profile |
|---|---|
| Size | 10–100 attorneys |
| Forensic capability | None in-house; relies on outside forensic vendors |
| Pain point | Vendor dependency for evidence handling creates risk and cost |
| Opportunity | Self-service evidence integrity without building internal forensic team |

---

## Disqualifying Characteristics

The following characteristics indicate a poor fit:

| Disqualifier | Reason |
|---|---|
| Requires SOC 2 Type II certification at contract signing | Not yet available; timeline documented in compliance roadmap |
| Requires on-premises deployment | Platform is cloud-hosted with documented security controls |
| Fewer than 5 matters per year | Volume does not justify platform adoption |
| Expects AI-driven evidence analysis | Platform provides integrity infrastructure, not analysis |
| Expects legal advice or case strategy from the platform | Platform is a technical tool, not a legal advisor |

## Buying Center

| Role | Function | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Managing Partner | Budget approval, strategic direction | Risk reduction, malpractice mitigation |
| IT Director / Administrator | Technical evaluation, integration | Security posture, deployment simplicity |
| Litigation Support Manager | Day-to-day use, workflow integration | Export reproducibility, audit trail access |
| Compliance / Risk Officer | Vendor assessment, regulatory alignment | Control documentation, SOC 2 roadmap |

---

## Qualification Criteria

A lead is considered qualified when:

1. Firm matches primary or secondary segment profile
2. At least one disqualifying characteristic is absent
3. A specific pain point related to evidence defensibility, export
   integrity, or audit trail credibility is identified
4. A named contact in the buying center has engaged (not just
   marketing interaction)
